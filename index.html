ABOUT US

The library can accommodate many students at any one time. The environment for learning is demonstrated by the 13 private study rooms and 38 group workstations available for group projects. Its collection ranges from books, Researches, Publishes periodicals. The collection also includes an expanding range of digital resources such as e-books, e-journals and online databases.

QIU extends access to UTM & UiTM e-libraries for its students, enriching their academic experience with a wealth of digital resources.

MISSION

To provide seamless access to a comprehensive range of academic resources, including the franchised e-resources of UTM & UiTM, as well as the E-Library of University of UTM & UiTM, empowering students at QIU with the tools and knowledge necessary for academic success and lifelong learning

VISION

To create a simple and accessible online platform where students can effortlessly discover and utilize the resources essential for their studies. Our aim is to foster a supportive digital space that encourages learning, exploration, and collaboration among students. We strive to empower students to thrive academically by offering them the necessary tools and assistance.

OBJECTIVE

  • To support the vision and mission of the University.
  • To ensure students can easily access the library's digital resources from anywhere, using any device.
  • To provide high-quality information services compatible with staff and student needs.

  • Students are required to display their ID card throughout their stay in the library.
  • Turn on the lights while using the study rooms.
  • Use of phone is restricted to silent mode only.
  • Eating, drinking (except for drinking water) and smoking are strictly prohibited in the library.
  • The library has the right to ask users whose behavior may be deemed as disturbing the peace of the library to leave the library.
  • Personal belongings should not be left unattended in the library at any time.
  • Misuse of the library computers is not allowed.
  • Tearing or damaging the library property/Books is strictly prohibited.
  • Littering, making noise, or causing disturbance to other library users are prohibited.
  • Students are allowed to borrow a maximum of 3 books at one time, and the loan period is 1 week from the date borrowed.
  • Renewal of the loan period can be done through the librarian before the due date.
  • All materials which have been lost or damaged should be reported immediately to the librarian. Borrowers need to replace the same book/latest edition OR pay the price of the book.
  • Final year students who fail to clear any fines or return materials borrowed from the library upon completion of their courses will have their transcript/result suspended.

The QIU Library offers an extensive selection of services in a space that is ideal for research and study, as well as convenient access to information resources.

  • Access to study spaces 36.5 hours a week.
  • 13 Private Study rooms for team working.
  • 25 Four Person Workstation tables.
  • 28 Study tables.
  • Books collection in various languages.
  • 2 franchised E-Resources of UTM & UITM.
  • More than 4 computers for E-learning.
  • Research assistance in person or by email.
  • Learning center for the Training and Development Center students.
  • Group Study Tables

    Comfortable study group areas are provided equipped with 18 study tables and chairs.

  • Four Single Study Workstations

    QIU Library provides 25 four single study workstations for its students.

  • Discussion/Study Rooms

    Thirteen study/discussion rooms are available in the library for individual or small group talks. There is space for four to eight people in the discussion room.

  • Computer

    Students have access to computer workstations at the library where they can use a variety of word processing and other applications, as well as online resources offered by the library. Students can use them to listen to lectures and other audio content that has been documented.

  • Wi-Fi Access

    The university provides wireless (Wi-Fi) connectivity within the library.
